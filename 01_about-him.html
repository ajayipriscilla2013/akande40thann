Pastor (Engr.) Moses Olukunmi Akande

Husband, Father, Grandfather
Pastor (Engr) Moses Olukunmi Akande was born in the 1950s to the family of the late Pa James Akande Odugade and the late Mrs. Monilola Akande (née Obadara), both natives of Ibadan. He attended St. Andrews Primary School, Bamigbola, in Lagelu Local Government Council Area, Ibadan, Oyo State, from 1964 to 1970.
He later proceeded to Anglican Modern School, Ojeedeji, in 1970 before continuing to Igboelerin Grammar School in 1971, graduating in 1976. He worked briefly as a teacher at Alafia Institute, Mokola, Ibadan, before joining WNTV/WNBS (now NTA Ibadan) as an Engineering Assistant.
He was admitted to The Polytechnic, Ibadan, in 1978 and graduated in 1982. He progressed through the Nigeria Society of Engineers, becoming a Graduate Member and later a Corporate Engineer. He pursued a Postgraduate Diploma in Mechanical Engineering at the Federal University of Technology, Akure.
In 1994, he gained admission to Lagos State University for his Master’s Degree in Business Management (MBA), which he completed in 1996. After graduating from The Polytechnic, Ibadan, in 1982, he completed his mandatory National Youth Service in Bauchi State, where he served at Nigeria Asbestos, a subsidiary of Hyderabad Industries, India. Due to his outstanding performance, he was retained by the company as a Plant Engineer.
He served the company for seven years, rising to the position of Assistant Chief Engineer. He was seconded to Hyderabad Industries in Hyderabad and New Delhi, India, and upon his return, he experienced rapid promotions due to his exceptional performance.
In 1989, he relocated to Lagos after securing employment with Lever Brothers PLC (now Unilever PLC) as an Engineering Manager. He rose to the position of Area Engineer before resigning in 1994. He then joined Consolidated Foods and Beverages Limited as the General Manager, leading a workforce of over 1,000 for 11 years.
In 2005, he briefly joined Monarchs Beverage and Foods Limited as the Managing Director for about three years before retiring to his own business, Kosedat Industries Limited, an engineering and manufacturing consulting firm. Throughout his career, he attended various trainings and conferences in India, Germany, Switzerland, and Great Britain.
His company has had contract opportunities with Chevron, and various food and manufacturing companies in Nigeria and the Republic of Benin. He is a professional engineer and corporate member of the Nigerian Institute of Management (MNSE and MNIM).
Due to his career and professional abilities, he has been a resource person to the Nigeria Society of Engineers (NSE) at both local and national levels and has delivered lectures at the Nigerian Physics Body held at LASU.
He served as a council member of the Manufacturing Association of Nigeria (MAN) Apapa Branch for five years.
Pastor Moses Akande was born again in 1974 through the activities of the Scripture Union of Nigeria.
He joined the Gospel Faith Mission International (GOFAMINT) in 1983 in Bauchi through Brother Ranti Ogunriola, now Pastor (Prof) Ranti Olunriola, who became a teacher while Pastor Moses Akande was a choir master.
Pastor Moses Akande holds a Postgraduate Diploma in Theology from the Redeemed Christian Bible College. After relocating to Lagos in 1989, Pastor Moses Akande continued with GOFAMINT briefly at Amuwo and Akowonjo Assemblies before settling in Agege, Lagos, and joining GOFAMINT Agege Assembly for proximity.
At Agege, he was a teacher in the special class, which later transformed into Ikeja Assembly, pastored by Pastor Engr. Tunde Ojo. Due to his relocation to Okokomaiko, Lagos, he joined GOFAMINT Okokomaiko Assembly, where he was made a Deacon and later an Elder. In 2006, he was posted to Barracks Assembly as the Pioneer Pastor-in-Charge. The assembly was inaugurated by Pastor H.I.D. Oluseye (then Regional Pastor of Lagos) and Pastor Yusuf (then District Pastor of Amuwo).
Over the years, Barracks Assembly gave birth to Tedi Assembly, with Pastor Idowu Eyekole as the Pastor-in-Charge. Pastor Moses Akande has acted as District Pastor at both District and Regional levels. In July 2018, he was installed as the Pastor-in-Charge of Victory Estate Assembly, where he remains till date.
In January 2019, he was appointed as the Assistant District Pastor of Amuwo District. Volkswagen Assembly was carved out of Victory Estate Assembly in March 2019. In 2021, he became the Amuwo District Pastor. He was appointed Chairman of the GOFAMINT Press and Bookshop Board, overseeing all printing and bookshop management, including the Beverages Division of GOFAMINT.
He was also made Chairman of GOFAMINT Region 8 Women's Ministry. In GOFAMINT, he has served as Chairman of the Assembly/District Development Committee and as the Regional Coordinator for the Gospel Business Men/Women Fellowship.
He has also assisted the National Gospel Businessmen Fellowship, where Pastor Engr. Onayemi coordinated the National Workshop in Ibadan. Pastor Moses Akande is happily married to his God-given helper and supporter, Mrs. Dorcas Akande, a retired professional teacher with the Federal Ministry of Education as an Assistant Director.
Their marriage is blessed with five children: Olubunmi, Oluwaseun, Oluwatoyin, Opeoluwa, and Joshua, all university graduates in engineering, science, and medicine. Four of their children are happily married. To God be the glory.
